In the early days of robotics, machines were very simple and limited in what they could do. One of the first real robots, Unimate, was created in the 1950s and used in factories.


These early robots operated by following fixed instructions programmed by humans. They did not think or learn—they simply repeated the same movements over and over. Most of them used mechanical arms powered by electricity or hydraulics to lift, move, or assemble parts.


They were mainly used for dangerous or repetitive tasks, helping workers stay safe and making production faster. Even though they were basic, these early robots laid the foundation for the advanced robots we have today.


Extra Information About Unimate:

Unimate was the world’s first industrial robot, invented by George Devol and brought to life by Joseph Engelberger. It was first installed in 1961 at a General Motors factory. Unimate was a large robotic arm that could lift heavy metal parts, move them, and perform repetitive tasks that were dangerous for humans. It used hydraulics, motors, and a magnetic memory drum to store instructions and repeat movements automatically.
